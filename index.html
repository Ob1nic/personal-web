About me

Architect by training, problem solver by nature, and a black belt in karate. I’m driven by the combination of logic and creativity. I finished high school at 16 and had to choose what to study in college. In truth, I didn’t want to study anything, as nothing aligned with my sense of purpose at the time. Since that purpose felt distant, I had to choose between physics/astronomy, architecture, and computer science.

Although basic programming had been part of my life since I was 13, I felt it was something I could explore on my own without needing a degree. Architecture became my path, initially without much conviction. The first semesters didn’t आकर्ष me, and I even considered dropping out, thinking it still wasn’t what I wanted to do.

That changed in my third semester, when I discovered the true potential of architecture as a fusion of mathematics and art. However, it was very stressful—many sleepless nights, countless cups of coffee, a drink I still consume today for its stimulating effect. During my architecture studies, I applied to an American university for computer science, but due to personal issues I wasn’t able to go to the United States. Back to logic…

Over time, I began to merge disciplines: applying architectural thinking to programming and computational logic to design. Today, I create digital products—some open, others developed to solve specific problems—while also working with sustainable and technological architecture. In addition, I explore physics, astronomy, and astrophysics, and I write about science, culture, and ideas.

Whether designing, programming, or teaching, I’m driven by curiosity and the desire to turn ideas into reality.

Working

I recently started the Weirdest Freakers Club, a project focused on building apps, software, and digital products. For now, it’s just me—no investors, no big team—driven by curiosity, stubbornness, and willpower. One day it may grow into a team, but every line of code, every decision, and every text exists because someone genuinely cares about what’s being built. It’s not a corporation—it’s a start. Technology designed to make life easier, created by people who are still building, not pretending they’ve already arrived. If this becomes something big, great. If not, at least it was done with purpose. Every product I create, whether free or addressing a specific need, comes from a genuine desire to solve problems and make an impact.
